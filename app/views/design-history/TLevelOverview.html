What are T Levels

T Levels are new courses which follow GCSEs and are equivalent to 3 A levels. These 2-year courses, which launched September 2020, have been developed in collaboration with employers and businesses so that the content meets the needs of industry and prepares students for work, further training or study.

T Levels offer students a mixture of classroom learning and ‘on-the-job’ experience during an industry placement of at least 315 hours (approximately 45 days).

Structure of a T Level

T Level courses include the following compulsory elements:

  • a technical qualification, which includes:
    • core theory, concepts and skills for an industry area
    • specialist skills and knowledge for an occupation or career
  • an industry placement with an employer

Students will also be required to work towards the attainment of maths and English if they have not already achieved grade 4 at GCSE, as they do on other 16 to 19 programmes.

What is R&C

Results and certification also known internally as R&C, is the data collection arm of the T Levels services. R&C is made up of two services for each of our end users: Providers and Awarding organisations (AO).

Data collected via the service include:

  • T Levels and course details that AO's will be providing
  • List of providers offering T Levels
  • Learner registration details: Name, ULN, date of birth, provider, T Level and specialism
  • Assessment entries for each individual learner i.e.
  • Grades for each above assessment entry
  • Learners level 2 attainment level for maths and English
  • Learners industry placement status
  • Providers organisational address (where statement of achivements and certificates will be sent to).

Using these details ESFA will calculate each individual learners T Level grade and issues a certificate.

Goals of R&C

  1. Registration - To ensure AO's and providers have the ability to register both T Levels and students respectively
  2. Results - To ensure T Levels students will receive accurate and timely results, including overall T Level grade.
  3. Certification - To ensure students are issued with statements of achievement and certification correctly describing their attainment
  4. Presentation - To ensure the way in which results, statement of achievements and certification are presented is clear to students, providers and end-users of the service
